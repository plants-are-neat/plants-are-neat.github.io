Why Plants + Data Are Amazing!

Excessive soil moisture can increase the risk of certain crop diseases. Knowing how wet the soil is can prevent overwatering.

On average, agriculture accounts for 70% of all freshwater withdrawals globally. Knowing exactly when crops need water can lead to better water conservation.

Agriculture is one of the most hazardous industries to work in. An average of 100 agricultural workers suffer a lost-work-time injury every day. Remote data tracking makes the lives of farmers safer and easier.

Sudden fluctuations in temperature heighten risk of crop failure. Knowing current field conditions, farmers can take the appropriate steps to protect their crops.

Farmers can analyze data trends, identify inefficiencies, and take steps to boost yields.

The average farm size in the US is 444 acres (around 340 football fields). Being able to know field conditions without stepping outside saves time and money.

Local farmers are being wiped out by industrial-scale practices. The US lost upwards of 100,000 farms between 2011 and 2018. We hope to help them save time, money, and resources to keep them afloat among competitors.

Farms that are very large can experience different conditions in different areas. Readings like barometric pressure can help predict weather patterns that may affect growing conditions.

Soil temperature is a key factor in choosing when to plant crops. Our product readily provides this information so farmers can plant their crops at the best possible time, for the best possible yields.
